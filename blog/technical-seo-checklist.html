Technical SEO Checklist: Mobile Speed & Core Web Vitals

Published April 21, 2026 • 8 min read

Technical SEO is the foundation. Even with perfect citations and reviews, a slow, broken website tanks your rankings. This checklist ensures your site meets Google's technical standards for local SEO.

Focus on these 5 critical areas for immediate ranking improvements.

1. Core Web Vitals (CWV)

Google penalizes slow sites. Your Core Web Vitals must be "Good":

  • Largest Contentful Paint (LCP): <2.5 seconds
  • First Input Delay (FID): <100 milliseconds
  • Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS): <0.1

How to check: PageSpeed Insights (insights.pagespeed.google.com)

How to improve:

  • Compress images (use WebP format)
  • Remove unused CSS/JavaScript
  • Enable lazy loading for images
  • Upgrade hosting if needed

2. Mobile-First Design

90% of local searches happen on mobile. Your site MUST work perfectly on phones:

  • Responsive design (not separate mobile site)
  • Readable without zooming
  • Touch buttons are large (48px+)
  • No horizontal scrolling
  • Fast on 3G connections

Check: Mobile-Friendly Test (search.google.com/test/mobile-friendly)

3. On-Page Technical Elements

  • Title tags: 50-60 characters, keyword at start
  • Meta descriptions: 150-160 characters, compelling CTA
  • H1 tag: One per page, matches page topic
  • Header hierarchy: H1 → H2 → H3 (no skipping)
  • Image alt text: Descriptive, keyword-relevant
  • Internal linking: Relevant anchor text, logical structure

4. Site Structure & Architecture

  • URL structure: /blog/topic-name (not /p=123)
  • Breadcrumbs: Home > Blog > Article
  • Sitemap: XML sitemap submitted to Google
  • Robots.txt: Proper crawl directives
  • Canonical tags: Prevent duplicate content

5. Schema Markup

Schema markup helps Google understand your content. Add:

  • LocalBusiness schema: Name, address, phone, hours
  • FAQPage schema: Your FAQ section
  • Article schema: Blog posts (datePublished, author, etc.)
  • AggregateRating schema: Your average review rating

Check: Google Rich Results Test (search.google.com/test/rich-results)

6. HTTPS & Security

  • SSL certificate installed (HTTPS, not HTTP)
  • No mixed content warnings
  • Security headers configured

7. Crawlability & Indexing

  • No "noindex" tags on important pages
  • Sitemap updated and submitted
  • No 404 errors on important pages
  • Redirect chains fixed (max 1 redirect)

Quick Wins This Week

  • Run PageSpeed Insights, fix low-hanging fruit
  • Add schema markup to homepage
  • Optimize 3-5 key images
  • Ensure mobile responsive design
  • Create/update robots.txt and sitemap
